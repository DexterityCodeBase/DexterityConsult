Case Study 3

Dexterity Streamlines Sachet Water Delivery and Saves the Company's Reputation and Boost their Bottom Line

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Everpure Ghana, a thriving producer of purified drinking water in Ghana started taking a hit on their reputation due to persistent incidents of late delivery of sachet water. This delay led to unhappy customers, eroded trust, and tarnished their business image and this took more than a toll on their business performance and profitability. It was in the midst of these daunting challenges and a literal fight for survival that Dexterity Consult was engaged to confront the issues.

OUR APPROACH

We started out by laying out the project's mission, scope, and goals which takes into account the desired outcome of all stakeholders. Afterwards, the data we collected from order placement to final delivery was a shock to everyone, even the process owners were astounded on the amount of redundant steps, unnecessary delays, and communication gaps identified in the existing process. For instance, we found that multiple manual checks were causing unnecessary delays in order processing. It was a complex maze of activities involving customer service, logistics, and quality control. We employed a Fishbone diagram and the "5 Whys" technique to systematically drill down into the underlying issues. To prioritize which issues needed immediate attention, we utilized Pareto analysis, identifying the vital few among the trivial many. Amongst other interventions we implemented several changes. We introduced an automated order processing system that reduced the chances of human error. We also streamlined their logistics by optimizing delivery routes and introducing real-time tracking. Quality control was another major focus. The team set strict quality standards and implemented checks at various stages of production and delivery to ensure that every sachet of water met those standards.

RESULTS

The results were nothing short of impressive. The company's delivery turnaround time began to shrink noticeably. What once took hours now took minutes, and their average delivery time reduced from 3.405 days to a swift 2.29 days, marking a remarkable 33% improvement rate. Customer satisfaction soared, and word of their improved service spread like wildfire. As the trends show, they are confident in a drastically increased sales regime, projecting a potential revenue boost of GHS 135,000.00 in 2024, as they set out to acquire an average of 60 new customers for every 1500 bags sold.

SUMMARY & PROJECT STATS

The challenge that necessitated this project at Everpure Ghana was delivery turnaround time which was far from ideal. It often took more than three days, and sometimes even exceeding four days, for orders to reach thirsty customers. Frustrated by this bottleneck, the company's leadership brought Dexterity Consult with hopes of turning their delivery service into a well-oiled machine, they embarked on a journey with us, toward transformation. In the end, what had once been a struggling sachet water delivery company had transformed into a steadily rising and reliable company with a rebranded reputation. Thanks to the disciplined application of Six Sigma principles in the masterful hands of Dexterity Consult and the Everpure dynamic leadership. The success of their Six Sigma journey didn't stop at faster delivery times. It led to cost savings, reduced waste, and improved employee morale. The company's reputation as a reliable and efficient sachet water provider grew, attracting even more customers.


At the end of the project we realized the following outcomes.
Primary Metric: Average delivery time reduced from 3.405 days to a swift 2.29 days
Secondary Metric: Quality control and new standards implemented. Enforcing checks at various stages of production and delivery to ensure that every sachet of water met those standards thereby reducing reworks and rejected deliveries.

Revenue Generation: GHS 135,000.00
