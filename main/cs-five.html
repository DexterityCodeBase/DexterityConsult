Case Study 5

A Quest to Overcome persistent Gasoline Shortages

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Over the course of six months, meticulous data analysis brought to light a concerning trend at a specific facility— a significant 1.19% scarcity in gasoline product. This shortfall translated into a monthly loss of 12,230 liters and, cumulatively, a staggering annual revenue deficit of approximately US$150,000. This revelation unveiled not only a critical financial challenge but also a systemic inefficiency that demanded immediate attention and strategic intervention. The impact of this persistent gasoline scarcity extended beyond the balance sheets, as it directly undermined the operational efficiency and profitability of the facility in question. The tangible loss in revenue underscored the urgent need for a comprehensive solution to reclaim the lost liters, fortify inventory management processes, and safeguard against future financial setbacks.

OUR APPROACH

We employed a multi-faceted approach. First, we established a master verification center at the primary depot to oversee the entire process. We also introduced containment protocols to counter fuel volatility, redesigned our bulk road vehicle (BRV) load-sharing system, implemented route reconnaissance and tracking mechanisms to prevent tampering, and conducted a controlled trial with real-time volume validation using flowmeters during fuel discharge. Next, we redesign the bulk road vehicle (BRV) load-sharing framework. By optimizing how fuel was distributed among BRVs, we aimed to minimize losses during transportation. Finally, we recognized that the human element was just as critical as the technology we deployed. Our comprehensive training program ensured that all personnel were well-versed in the standard operating procedures (SOPs) governing fuel discharge and master verification.

RESULTS

By systematically addressing root causes, our project successfully mitigated fuel distribution inconsistencies, leading to increased efficiency and significant cost savings. These protocols prevented vaporization and further loss during transit and storage. As a result of this meticulous tool deployment, our pilot initiatives delivered impressive results. Fuel shortages reduced dramatically, averaging just 0.50% per delivery. This achievement translated to an impressive annual cost savings potential of US$87,500, preserving 7,000 liters of fuel monthly, heralding a new era of efficiency and savings.

SUMMARY & PROJECT STATS

A Six Sigma approach was employed to address the persistent gasoline shortages at 29 fuel stations. Data analysis identified a 1.19% scarcity in gasoline, resulting in a monthly loss of 12,230 liters and an annual revenue loss of approximately US$150,000. The Six Sigma methodology was applied to improve processes, reduce waste, and enhance efficiency, ultimately mitigating the gasoline shortages and minimizing revenue loss.


At the end of the project we realized the following outcomes.
Primary Metric: Reduction in fuel shortages (1.19% to 0.50% per delivery) preserving 7,000 liters of fuel monthly.
Secondary Metric: Prevention of product vaporization and loss during transit and storage, including theft.
Financial Metric:
Cost Savings: US$87,500 annual cost savings.
Cost Avoidance: Reduction in monthly financial deficit of US$12,500. (approximately US$150,000.00 annually).
Revenue Generation: N/A

Qualitative Metrics: Improved trust and job satisfaction.
