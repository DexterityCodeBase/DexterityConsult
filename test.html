Case Study

PROBLEM STATMENT

The intersection of fault planes, compounded by the influence of underground water, has given rise to a disconcerting scenario at a vital pit site. A consistent 20mm displacement along the perimeter of the pit has emerged, a stark deviation from the industrial standard of 5mm.
This alarming disparity not only poses an imminent threat to the safety of mine workers but also carries a significant financial risk. The persistent 20mm displacement raises grave concerns about the stability of the pit walls, heightening the likelihood of a potential failure. Such an occurrence could have severe consequences, endangering the lives of on-site personnel and precipitating a dire financial risk. The strategically important nature of the pit site amplifies the potential impact, with an estimated financial risk looming at a staggering $5.9 million..

OUR APPROACH

To tackle this issue, we employed a set of powerful tools and methodologies, including D-M-Kaizen-C. First, as usual we established what was critical to both internal and external customers and their very implications to business and then went on to conduct extensive measurement activities, improving data precision through Gage R&R analysis and using run charts and principal component analysis (PCA). We implemented innovative measures such as sinking boreholes and creating weep holes while redesigning the slope angle from 60 to 30 degrees. These solutions underwent Failure Modes, Effects, and Criticality Analysis (FMECA) and were replicated successfully. Additionally, we closely monitored Prism data with control charts.

RESULTS

Over a span of three months, our efforts yielded remarkable results. An analysis of Prism data and a process capability assessment before and after our interventions revealed the complete elimination of Out-of-Specification (OOS) occurrences. We literally annihilated an initial OOS rate of 68.89% to 0.00%.

This achievement not only enhanced safety but also brought substantial economic benefits. With an estimated implementation cost of $211,000, we averted potential wall collapses, potentially saving $6.2 million in revenue for each avoided incident. Furthermore, we harnessed extracted groundwater for dust suppression, leading to an additional annual operational expenditure reduction of $165,000.

SUMMARY & PROJECT STATS

On this project of Reducing Pit wall instability. We found that the culprit was the convergence of fault planes, exacerbated by the relentless influence of subterranean water. A noticeable displacement of 20mm along the pit perimeter, far from the industry standard of 5mm, was a cause for concern.


At the end of the project we realized the following outcomes.
Primary Metric: Reduced Out-of-Specification (OOS) Pit Wall Failure occurrences from 68.89% to 0.00%.
